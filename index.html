Martin Tušl, PhD

Occupational Health Psychologist

Work takes up a large part of our lives and it can either support or undermine our well-being. I help individuals and organizations to identify and understand what can make their work fulfilling, healthy, and sustainable, drawing on decades of scientific research in occupational health psychology.

My approach is grounded in science but stays close to people. I believe that good research should translate into real change, for individuals and for the organizations they work in.

Who I work with

Individuals

People navigating burnout, career transitions, work-life balance, or a search for more meaning in their work.

Organizations & teams

Companies looking to build healthier, more engaged workplaces through evidence-based approaches to job design and well-being.

Work with me

Evidence-based support for individuals and organizations

Whether you are an individual looking for support in your professional life, or an organization seeking to improve well-being at work, I offer a thoughtful, science-informed approach tailored to your needs.

For individuals

I work with individuals on questions of meaning, balance, and well-being at work. Sessions draw on frameworks from occupational health psychology, including job crafting, psychological needs theory, and salutogenesis, to help you understand your situation and find practical ways forward.

For organizations

I collaborate with teams and companies on workplace well-being, job design, and organizational health. This can take the form of workshops, assessments, or longer-term consulting projects, always grounded in current research and adapted to your organizational context.
